Arroz con Gandules

History

Starting in 1900, immigrants from Puerto Rico were recruited to work on Hawaii's sugar plantations. One of the plants they brought over to Hawaii was the large shrub called Gandule, which bore a bean used in many Puerto Rican dishes. Arroz con Gandule is a celebratory dish usually reserved for holidays. And like many cultural dishes that made their way to Hawaii, recipes were altered because traditional ingredients were not available in Hawaii at the time.

Recipe Description

There's nothing more quintessentially Puerto Rican than arroz con gandules. It’s part of our national dish. For holidays, birthdays, baby showers, and more, if there’s a party, this dish will be there.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups long-grain rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) gandules (pigeon peas), drained
  • 2 tablespoons sofrito
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cups water or chicken broth
  • 1 packet of sazón with annatto
  • 1 teaspoon adobo seasoning
  • 1/2 cup diced ham or bacon (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat and sauté sofrito and diced ham or bacon until fragrant.
  2. Stir in tomato sauce, sazón, adobo, and pigeon peas, cooking for 2 minutes.
  3. Add rice to the pot, stirring to coat it with the seasonings.
  4. Pour in water or chicken broth, bring to a boil, and then reduce heat to low.
  5. Cover and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and liquid is absorbed.
  6. Fluff rice with a fork and serve hot.

Cooking Tips

  • For added flavor, use homemade chicken broth instead of water.
  • Top with fresh cilantro or sliced olives for a garnish.
